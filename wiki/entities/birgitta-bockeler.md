## Summary

Birgitta Böckeler is a technologist at Thoughtworks who writes on Martin Fowler's site about software engineering with AI. She authored [[harness-engineering-coding-agent-users]], the originating mental model that frames the user-built outer harness around a coding agent in terms of feedforward **guides** and feedback **sensors**, split further along a computational vs inferential axis. [[harness-engineering-coding-agent-users]]

## Key Points

- Coined / popularized the guides-and-sensors framing for harness engineering, and the view of the harness as a [cybernetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cybernetics) governor regulating a codebase toward a desired state. [[harness-engineering-coding-agent-users]]
- Distinguishes three regulation categories: maintainability, architecture fitness, and behaviour harness, noting the behaviour harness is the hardest open problem. [[harness-engineering-coding-agent-users]]
- Her work is cited by Addy Osmani in [[agent-harness-engineering]] as one of the sources behind the broader `agent = model + harness` discipline. [[agent-harness-engineering]]
- Published an initial harness-engineering memo in February 2026; the April 2026 article supersedes it. [[harness-engineering-coding-agent-users]]
