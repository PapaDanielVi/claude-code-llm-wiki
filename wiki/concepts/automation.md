## Summary

Automation is the creation and deployment of systems that operate without continuous human input. Traditional automation handles rule-based, repetitive tasks — if X, then do Y. Agentic automation extends this to cognitive tasks: the system perceives, reasons, decides, and acts based on context and goals.

## Key Points

- **Scope**: From simple scripts → RPA → agentic workflows (increasing autonomy)
- **Trigger types**: Manual, scheduled, event-driven (webhooks, API calls)
- **Self-healing**: Advanced automation can detect failures and recover without human intervention
- **Business impact**: Automation of cognitive work is the "next stage of the economy"

## Relationship to Agentic Workflows

Agentic workflows are a form of advanced automation where the "automation engine" is an AI agent that can:

- Handle ambiguity and context-dependent decisions
- Use tools and APIs dynamically
- Self-correct when errors occur
- Scale to complex, multi-step processes
